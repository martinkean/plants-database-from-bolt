Discover Aotearoa's Native Flora

Explore New Zealand's unique and diverse native plant species, from ancient kauri trees to delicate alpine flowers. Learn about the cultural significance and ecological importance of Aotearoa's botanical treasures.

Pōhutukawa

New Zealand Christmas tree with vibrant red flowers that bloom in summer, creating spectacular coastal displays around the country.

Fern

Silver Fern / Ponga

National symbol of New Zealand with distinctive silver undersides on fronds, used as a natural compass by early Māori.

Mānuka

Famous for producing medicinal honey, this hardy shrub produces masses of white or pink flowers and thrives in diverse conditions.

Tree

Kauri

Ancient giants that can live over 1,000 years, these magnificent trees are among New Zealand's most iconic and culturally significant species.

Learn More
Tree

Kōwhai

New Zealand's national flower featuring stunning golden-yellow blooms that appear in spring, providing nectar for native birds like tui.

Learn More
Shrub

Harakeke / Flax

Traditional weaving material with strong, flexible leaves used by Māori for centuries to create baskets, mats, and clothing.

Learn More
Fern

Punga / Tree Fern

Prehistoric canopy creators with massive fronds that can reach up to 10 meters, creating magical forest environments.

Learn More
Tree

Tōtara

Slow-growing conifer with immense cultural significance, traditionally used for carving waka and building, can live over 1,800 years.

Learn More
Tree

Rātā

Epiphytic trees that begin life on other trees, eventually becoming magnificent specimens with brilliant red flowers beloved by native birds.

Learn More
Shrub

Hebe

Over 100 species with varied flowers from white to deep purple, these hardy shrubs are essential pollinators for native insects.

Learn More
Tree

Nikau Palm

New Zealand's only native palm, found in coastal and lowland forests, with distinctive fan-shaped leaves and orange fruit clusters.

Shrub

Heart-shaped leaves with powerful medicinal properties, traditionally used by Māori for treating ailments and still valued in herbal medicine today.
